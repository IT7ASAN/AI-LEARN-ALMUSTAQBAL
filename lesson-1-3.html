English Literacy Course

Unit 3 – Daily Routines

Learn vocabulary for daily activities and how to talk about your daily routine.

🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Understand and use vocabulary for common daily activities
  • Talk about your daily routine using simple sentences
  • Practice sequencing activities in the correct order

🗓️ 1. Vocabulary: Daily Activities

Common daily activities:

Daily Activities and Examples
Activity Example Sentence
Wake up I wake up at 7:00 AM.
Brush teeth I brush my teeth after I wake up.
Eat breakfast I eat breakfast at 8:00 AM.
Go to school I go to school at 9:00 AM.
Study I study in the classroom.
Eat lunch I eat lunch at 1:00 PM.
Go home I go home at 3:00 PM.
Watch TV I watch TV in the evening.
Eat dinner I eat dinner at 7:00 PM.
Go to bed I go to bed at 9:00 PM.

✅ Activity:

Point to the correct picture for each action:

Wake up

Brush teeth

Eat breakfast

Go to school

🧩 2. Order of Activities

Put these activities in the correct order:

  1. Wake up

✅ Practice:

Use pictures or flashcards to arrange activities in order.

👄 3. Speaking Practice

Use simple sentences to talk about your routine:

  • "I wake up at 7:00."
  • "I eat breakfast at 8:00."
  • "I go to school at 9:00."

✅ Activity:

Tell your routine in 3–4 steps.

👥 4. Short Dialogue

Example conversation:

A:
What do you do in the morning?
B:
I brush my teeth and eat breakfast.

✅ Activity:

Practice with a partner.

✏️ 6. Writing Practice

Write or trace the following:

Wake up

Eat breakfast

Go to school

Go to bed

✅ Encourage repetition and drawing the activity.

📋 8. Recap & Homework

Today we learned:

  • Vocabulary for daily routines
  • How to describe your routine
  • How to order your activities

📝 Homework:

  1. Draw and label 3 activities you do every day
  2. Tell your family your daily routine in English
