English Literacy Course

Unit 3 – Time and Daily Schedules

Learn to tell time and describe your daily routine in English.

🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Tell time using "o'clock" and "half past"
  • Use daily routine vocabulary in context
  • Create and describe your own daily schedule

📝 2. Grammar Focus

📌 Present Simple for Routines

I wake up at 7 o'clock.
She eats lunch at 1:30.

✅ Activity

Fill in the blanks:

  1. He _____ at 8:00. (go to school)
  2. We _____ at 7:30. (eat breakfast)
  3. She _____ at 9:00. (go to bed)

📖 3. Reading Practice

📄 Reading Paragraph

My name is Omar. I wake up at 6:00 in the morning. I go to school at 8:00. I eat lunch at 1:00. I go home at 3:00 and sleep at 9:00.

✅ Comprehension Questions

  1. What time does Omar wake up?
  2. When does he go to sleep?

✍️ 4. Writing Practice

📝 Example

I wake up at 7:00. I go to school at 8:30. I eat lunch at 1:00.

🗣️ 5. Speaking Practice

💬 Pair Practice Example

A: What time do you wake up?
B: I wake up at 6:30.
A: What do you do at 1:00?
B: I eat lunch.

✅ Activity

Use flashcards showing clocks + daily actions to practice with a partner.

🎧 6. Listening Practice

✅ Task

Listen to daily routine descriptions and point to the correct time on a clock.

Example Prompts:

  1. "She goes to bed at 9:00."
  2. "He eats breakfast at 7:30."
