English Literacy Course

Unit 7 – Safety and Rules

Learn to recognize safety signs and follow important rules.

🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Recognize basic safety signs and warnings
  • Use imperative sentences to follow and give rules
  • Practice what to do in emergency situations

📝 2. Grammar Focus

📌 Imperative Sentences (used to give commands or instructions)

Stop!

Don't touch that!

Be careful.

Wear a helmet.

✅ Activity

Fill in the blanks:

_____ the road at the sign.

Don't _____ near the fire.

📖 3. Reading Practice

📄 Safety Notice

In case of fire, do not run. Use the stairs. Stay calm and follow the signs to the exit. Ask for help if needed.

✅ Comprehension Questions

  1. What should you do in a fire?
  2. What should you not do?

✍️ 4. Writing Practice

📝 Example Rules

  • Don't run in the hallway.
  • Wear shoes outside.
  • Cross the street carefully.

✅ Activity

Write 3 safety rules you follow at home or school:

🚫🏃

No running

⛑️

Wear helmet

🚸

Use crosswalk

🗣️ 5. Speaking Practice

💬 Roleplay Example

A:
Don't touch the fire.
B:
Okay, I won't.

✅ Activity

Students practice giving and following safety rules:

Try roleplaying a fire drill, street crossing, or school safety scene

🎧 6. Listening Practice

✅ Task

Teacher says safety commands → students act out or point to the correct picture:

🛑

Stop

🚶

Walk

🚪

Exit

Example Prompts:

  1. "Wear your seatbelt."
  2. "Walk, don't run!"
  3. "Stop at the red sign."

🔍 7. Fun Activity: Safety Sign Hunt

Instructions:

  • Place printed safety signs around the classroom or home
  • Students go on a "Safety Hunt"
  • Each time they find a sign, they must name it and say what it means

Encourage creativity:

Students can make their own signs using crayons or markers

🚫🏊

No swimming

💦

Wet floor

📌 8. Recap & Homework

Today We Learned:

  • Important safety vocabulary and signs
  • How to give and follow rules using imperatives
  • What to do in emergency situations

📝 Homework:

  1. Draw 2 safety signs and write what they mean
  2. Talk to someone at home about one safety rule you follow

Example: "We always wear seatbelts in the car."
