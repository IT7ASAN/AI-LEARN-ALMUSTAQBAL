English Literacy Course

Unit 4 – Numbers and Colors

Learn numbers from 1 to 10 and basic colors in English with fun activities.

🎯 Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Recognize and count numbers from 1 to 10
  • Identify and name basic colors
  • Use numbers and colors in simple sentences

Numbers and colors are everywhere in our daily life. Let's learn them together!

🔢 1. Numbers from 1 to 10

Let's learn the numbers together. Repeat after me:

Numbers and their words
Number Word Pronunciation
1 One /wʌn/
2 Two /tuː/
3 Three /θriː/
4 Four /fɔːr/
5 Five /faɪv/
6 Six /sɪks/
7 Seven /ˈsev.ən/
8 Eight /eɪt/
9 Nine /naɪn/
10 Ten /ten/

Practice counting: "One, two, three, four, five, six, seven, eight, nine, ten!"

✅ Activity:

Count with your fingers from 1 to 10. Hold up the correct number of fingers as you say each number.

Example: Say "one" and hold up 1 finger, say "two" and hold up 2 fingers, and so on.

🎨 2. Basic Colors

Colors make our world beautiful. Let's learn their names:

Red Apple
Blue Sky
Green Tree
Yellow Sun
Black Cat
White Cloud
Orange Carrot
Pink Flower
Purple Grapes
Brown Chocolate

Practice saying: "The apple is red. The sky is blue. The tree is green."

✅ Activity:

Point to things in the room with these colors. Say: "I see something [color]."

Example: "I see something red" (point to a red object).

👄 3. Speaking Practice

Now let's use numbers and colors together in simple sentences:

Examples:

"I see one red apple."
"I have two blue pens."
"She has three yellow flowers."
"There are four green trees."
"My bag has five black books."

Remember the pattern: Number + Color + Object

✅ Activity:

Each student says a sentence with a number and color.

Example: "I have three red pencils." or "I see two white clouds."

🎮 4. Game: Match Numbers and Colors

Let's play a fun matching game to practice what we've learned!

✅ Game Instructions:

🎵 6. Song Time

Let's sing along to practice numbers:

"One, Two, Buckle My Shoe"

One, two, buckle my shoe

Three, four, shut the door

Five, six, pick up sticks

Seven, eight, lay them straight

Nine, ten, a big fat hen!

This traditional counting rhyme helps us remember numbers in order.

✅ Activity:

Clap or tap along with the rhythm as we sing. Try doing actions for each line:

  • "buckle my shoe" - pretend to buckle a shoe
  • "shut the door" - pretend to close a door
  • "pick up sticks" - pretend to pick up sticks

📋 7. Recap & Homework

Today we learned:

  • Numbers from 1 to 10 and how to count objects
  • Basic color names and how to describe objects by color
  • How to use numbers and colors together in simple sentences

📝 Homework:

  1. Count 10 items at home and name their colors
  2. Draw and label five colored objects (example: draw an apple and write "red apple")
  3. Practice the "One, Two, Buckle My Shoe" rhyme with your family
